NFL Championship Era

The Origins of Professional Football in Washington, D.C.

Washington's NFL franchise has taken on many different looks throughout the years, but the first iteration was the Washington Redskins.

George Preston Marshall originally owned the professional team in Boston, but relocated to Washington, D.C. in 1937, thus birthing the Washington Redskins.

1937 also marked the debut of franchise legend Sammy Baugh. "Slinging Sammy" quarterbacked the Redskins to an NFL Championship in their first year of existence.

Baugh captained Washington to four more NFL Chamionship appearances in the next eight years, including a second title in 1942, all en route to a Hall of Fame career.

Over the next few decades, Washington was mostly stuck in mediocrity ... until the franchise was revitalized by Vince Lombardi during the dawn of the Super Bowl Era.
